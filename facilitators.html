Meet the Facilitators

Connect with award winning African writers and poets from across the globe on iWorder

Sibongile Fisher

Sibongile Fisher is a poet, writer and drama facilitator from Johannesburg, South Africa.

She holds a BCom degree in Marketing Management and a higher certificate in the Performing Arts and wishes to pursue an MA in Creative Writing. She is the co-founder of The Raising Zion Foundation, an arts organization that focuses on promoting literature, poetry and the performing arts in high schools.

She is also the winner of the 2016 Short Story Day Africa Prize for her short story “A Door Ajar”. “A Door Ajar” was also shortlisted for the Brittle Paper Literary Awards. Her short story “A sea of secrets” written for young adults was published by Fundza under their mentorship program and it appears in their “it takes two!” volume 2 anthology.

Gbenga Adesina

Gbenga Adesina, Nigerian born poet and essayist, jointly won the 2016 Brunel African Poetry Prize.

His poem, “How To Paint A Girl” was selected by Mathew Zapruder for its “clarity of observation and empathetic insight into the suffering of another” for the New York Times. He was a 2016 Norman Mailer Poetry Fellow at Pepperdine University, Malibu, California.

He has received scholarships and residencies from Fine Arts Work Centre, Provincetown and The Open Society Foundation in Goree Island, off the coast of Senegal. His poetry chapbook, Painter of Water was published by APBF and Akashic Books, New York in a series edited by Kwame Dawes and Chris Abani.

He recently joined NYU as a Starworks Fellow, where he teaches undergraduate poetry courses. His poems have appeared or forthcoming in Prairie Schooner, Brittle Paper, Vinyl, Ploughshares and elsewhere. His poetry manuscript, Holy Bodies was a finalist for the 2017 Sillerman First Book Prize.

Megan Ross

Megan Ross is a writer, poet and journalist from East London. Her fiction, nonfiction and poetry have appeared in Prufrock, Poetry Pacific, The Kalahari Review, New Contrast, New Coin, Itch, Glamour, GQ, Fairlady, O, The Oprah Magazine and the Mail and Guardian.

She is the winner of the Brittle Paper Award for Fiction, and is a runner up for both the Short Story Day Africa and Short Sharp Awards.

She is also an Iceland Writers Retreat Alumni Award winner and has been twice-shortlisted for the Miles Morland Scholarship. Her first book, Milk Fever, is forthcoming from uHlanga. Megan is mom to a very busy two-year-old and is most herself when in the Indian Ocean.

Adedayo Adeyemi Agarau

Adedayo Adeyemi Agarau is a Nigerian food nutritionist from the uphill area of Oke Agbo in Ijebu Igbo, Ogun state. He was born in Ibadan. Adedayo started writing poetry in 2013. He has won several writing awards including the ‘What Can Words Do’ challenge in 2013, the Pulse Student Poetry Prize and the Tony Tokunbo Fernandez International Poetry Prize in 2014, the Brigitte Pooirson Poetry Contest (July) in 2015 and the Eriata Food Poetry Contest in 2016. In 2013, he compiled a collection of political poetry, Epistle of Lies, which featured a hundred poets. His book, For Boys Who Went, was published in 2016 by Words, Rhymes and Rhythm.

Adedayo’s words have been also been published on Kalahari, Sankofa, WRR, African Writers, Praxis Magazine, and several other platforms.

He lives in and Writes from Ibadan, Nigeria.
